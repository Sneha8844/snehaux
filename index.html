Grovia – Helping Shoppers Find Their Way

No one dreams of wandering grocery aisles. Grovia was born out of a gap between in-store reality and digital expectations.

The Insight That Sparked It

It started with a casual complaint: "Why don’t they just have a ‘find item’ button in the store?" That frustration became Grovia — a smart, human shopping companion.

Research & Discovery

User Interviews

  • "I always forget just one item." (5/6 users)
  • "I use WhatsApp or Notes for lists." (4/6)
  • "I hate circling back." (5/6)
  • "Shared lists don’t update in real time." (3/6)

Journey Map (Renu)

  • Pre-store: List on paper → Mobile sync
  • In-store: Asks staff → AR guidance
  • Mid-trip: Checks cart → Auto check-off
  • Post-trip: Missed items → Confirmation & History

Information Architecture

4 flows: Home → Add Items → Shop (AR) → History

User Flow


[Home] ↓ [Search/Add Items] → [Edit/Share List] ↓ [Shopping Mode] → [AR Guidance] → [Auto Got It!] ↓ [Completed List] → [History] → [Reuse/Analyze]

Wireframes & Prototypes

From lo-fi wireframes to interactive flows with collapsible item info and aisle grouping.

Usability Testing

  • Tool: Maze | 5 users
  • Issues: History tab hidden → now in main tab
  • Skipped AR intro → reduced to 3 slides
  • Search hover confusion → added tooltip

A/B Testing

Tested subtle vs. pop-up feedback. Users preferred pop-up for visibility and satisfaction.

Iterations

  • Reduced start-to-shop steps
  • Added avatars for shared lists
  • ‘Find in Store’ now 1 tap away

Lessons Learned

  • Speed with control > automation
  • History = loyalty
  • Optional AR = less friction
